Q1 3 marks Geometry & Symmetry
Four of these shapes have one or more lines of symmetry. Which one does not?
AV
BW
CX
DY
EZ
Answer: E (Z)
The letter Z has rotational symmetry but no line of symmetry. V, W, X and Y each have at least one vertical line of symmetry (shown as dashed lines).
Q2 3 marks Logic & Problem Solving
Ten chairs are equally spaced around a round table, numbered 1 to 10 in order.
Which chair is opposite chair 9?
A1
B2
C3
D4
E5
Answer: D (4)
The opposite chair is 5 places forward and 5 places back. Counting backwards from chair 9: 8, 7, 6, 5, 4 — so chair 4 is opposite. Shown by the blue arrow.
Q3 4 marks Patterns & Sequences
Sally uses matchsticks and clay blobs to make cubes in a row. The first cube uses 12 matchsticks. Each new cube shares a face and needs only 8 more.

How many cubes can Sally make with exactly 60 matchsticks?
A5
B6
C7
D8
E9
Answer: C (7 cubes)
Formula: 12 + 8 × (n − 1) = 60
8 × (n − 1) = 48 → n − 1 = 6 → n = 7 cubes.
Q4 4 marks Logic & Problem Solving
Place the numbers 1 to 7 in the circles so each line of three adds to 12.
Which number must go in the centre?
A7
B6
C5
D4
E2
Answer: D (4)
The number 4 appears in 3 of the 5 possible sums that equal 12 (7+4+1, 6+4+2, 5+4+3). Since the centre belongs to 3 lines, 4 must go there.
Q5 4 marks Geometry & Symmetry
A circular paper is folded in half twice, then a cut is made in the corner.
When unfolded, what shape is the hole in the centre?
AA diamond ◆
BA square
CA triangle
DA star
EA rectangle
Answer: A (A diamond)
Each unfolding step reflects the cut through the fold line, creating a symmetrical diamond shape in the centre of the circle.
